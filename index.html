The Non-Linear Path: On Burnout, Bounces, and Building

Well, it’s been a minute. I wanted to check in and post an update from the front lines of building a new career, and let me tell you—the last few weeks have been a real testament to the fact that this path is anything but a straight line. If I'm being honest, I'm feeling pretty run-down at the moment. I'm nursing the tail-end of a fourth straight week of laryngitis, which is a lovely parting gift from a customer support job I tried out recently. It took only a week and a half to gather some very clear personal data: high-stress, micromanaged call center environments are absolutely not for me. Walking away from what could have been a steady job was tough, but it was one of the best decisions I could have made for my mental health. It was a detour, but a necessary and valuable one. And in a wild turn of events, just as that door closed, another one opened. I was so fortunate to land a project right after with an educational scoring company that I absolutely adore. The work is so fulfilling, and it was a much-needed boost for my finances and my spirit. If only it were a year-round opportunity instead of seasonal, it would honestly be a dream job! That project has since wrapped up, and I've been lucky enough to jump right into my next contract with a tech company I've worked with before. I'll be doing content review, writing, and data labeling until about the end of July, and I'm incredibly grateful to have these projects that keep everything moving forward. But I suppose the real story isn't just about hopping from one gig to the next. The real work is what happens in between, during the quiet hours. My brain is constantly trying to absorb everything I can. I'm pushing personal projects to GitHub, diving into AI ethics research, and working my way through courses on **365 Data Science**, **Maven Analytics**, and **DataCamp**. And through it all, I’m still plugging away at my biggest goal: learning more **Python**. It isn't easy, and some days (like today) I'm just plain tired. But every course I finish, every line of code I write, and every project I complete feels like laying another brick in the foundation of a future I'm building for myself—one that's more skilled, more marketable, and more authentically *me*. So, that’s the update from my little corner of the world. A little bit of burnout, a couple of lucky bounces, and a whole lot of building. Thanks for reading. ❤️

July 2, 2025

init: Starting The CodeByteQuill README

Hello, World! And just like that, it begins. This is the first post on my brand new blog, and if I'm being honest, it's both exciting and a little terrifying. This space is something I've wanted to create for a while: a personal log, a digital lab notebook, and a public record of my journey into the world of code and technology. This is my README file, for the project that is me. For a long time, I've been fascinated by how things work under the hood. I finally decided to stop wondering and start doing, and this blog is my first real step. It's my commitment to myself to learn in the open, share what I discover, and document the inevitable bugs, frustrations, and those amazing "aha!" moments along the way. The name of this blog, and my username **CodeByteQuill**, is something I put a lot of thought into. It’s a summary of its mission here: * **Code & Byte:** For the technical, digital, world I'm diving into. * **Quill:** For the timeless, human act of writing and journaling. It’s a nod to my own name and a reminder that learning to code is also a creative craft. What you're looking at right now is my very first project. I built this simple blog app from scratch with the help of my AI brainstorming partner, Gemini. It’s a single HTML file that uses JavaScript to save posts to the browser's local storage, all wrapped in a clean, dark-mode theme. We even added edit and delete functions! Deploying it to Netlify felt like a huge victory, and every little feature we added felt like unlocking a new level. So here it is. The first commit. The project has been initialized. I have no doubt this journey will be challenging, but I'm ready for it. Thanks for stopping by. I can't wait to see what we build next.
